# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in
the Velune community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of
age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Be respectful and considerate.
- Give actionable, technically grounded feedback.
- Accept constructive feedback without personal attacks.
- Respect differing experiences and points of view.
- Take responsibility, apologize when appropriate, and learn from mistakes.
- Focus on what is best for the project and its community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, threats, stalking, or intimidation.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Trolling, insulting comments, or personal and political attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Sustained disruption of project discussions or collaboration.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional
  setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of
Conduct. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to
behavior they determine to be inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or
harmful.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, documentation,
issues, and other contributions that are inconsistent with this Code of
Conduct. When appropriate, they will communicate the reason for a moderation
decision.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions,
reviews, project events, and other spaces where a person represents Velune.
Representation includes using an official project account, posting through an
official channel, or acting as an appointed project representative.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior privately
through GitHub private vulnerability reporting:

<https://github.com/kxpw/Velune/security/advisories/new>

Begin the report title with `Community conduct report` so maintainers can
separate it from security reports. Include the relevant URLs, dates, a factual
description, and any supporting material that can be shared safely. Do not
include unrelated vulnerability data.

If private reporting is unavailable, open a public issue containing only a
request for a private contact channel. Do not include names, incident details,
or evidence in that issue.

Every report will be reviewed promptly and fairly. Information will be shared
only with people needed to investigate and resolve the report, except when
disclosure is required by law or necessary to protect someone from immediate
harm. Retaliation against a person who makes a good-faith report or
participates in an investigation is prohibited.

A maintainer who is named in a report or has a conflict of interest must recuse
themselves. The remaining maintainers will appoint an impartial reviewer when
needed.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will use these guidelines when determining consequences. Context,
severity, impact, prior behavior, and willingness to repair harm may affect the
response.

### 1. Correction

**Community impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior judged
unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private written correction explaining the violation and how
to avoid repeating it. A public clarification or apology may be requested when
the harm occurred publicly.

### 2. Warning

**Community impact:** A violation through a single incident or a series of
actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with conditions for continued participation. The
person may be asked to avoid contact with specified participants or spaces for
a defined period. Violating those conditions may lead to a temporary or
permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community impact:** A serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from project interaction and public
communication for a defined period. Attempts to evade the ban may lead to a
permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community impact:** A pattern of violations, harassment of an individual, or
aggression toward or disparagement of a class of people.

**Consequence:** Permanent removal from project spaces and participation.

## Appeals

A person subject to an enforcement action may request one reconsideration
through the same private reporting channel. The request must explain the
factual or procedural basis for reconsideration. A maintainer who made the
original decision will not be the sole reviewer of the appeal. Decisions made
after reconsideration are final.
